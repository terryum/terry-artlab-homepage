## TL;DR

The first autonomous research framework specialized for clinical medicine, using clinician-engineer co-reasoning to generate evidence-grounded ideas and automating experiments and manuscript drafting, achieving higher idea quality than commercial LLMs and producing MICCAI-level papers.

## Problem

AI Scientist systems that autonomously generate hypotheses, conduct experiments, and write manuscripts have emerged, but existing systems remain domain-agnostic, limiting their applicability to clinical medicine. Medical research requires grounding in medical evidence, handling specialized data modalities (CT, MRI, pathology, etc.), and adherence to ethical policies. This paper asks whether an AI scientist can automate the full clinical research pipeline with evidence-based rigor.

## Key Idea

Medical AI Scientist is a multi-agent framework built on three core design principles:

- **Clinically grounded ideation**: A **clinician-engineer co-reasoning** mechanism transforms extensively surveyed literature into actionable evidence, ensuring traceability of generated research ideas
- **3 research modes**: (1) Paper-based Reproduction, (2) Literature-inspired Innovation, (3) Task-driven Exploration — each with progressively increasing autonomy
- **Evidence-grounded manuscript drafting**: Guided by structured medical compositional conventions and ethical policies

The agent architecture comprises **Idea Proposer** (Explorer, Surveyor, Planner, Idea Generator, Assessor), **Experimental Executor** (Coder, Debugger, Analyst), and **Manuscript Composer** (Stylist, Formatter, LaTeX Compiler), leveraging MONAI medical tools, Dockerized Sandbox, and PubMed/IEEE Xplore knowledge bases.

<Collapsible title="Detailed Method">

### Idea Proposer

Idea generation involves multi-agent collaboration:

1. **Explorer**: Core challenge analysis and paradigm matching
2. **Surveyor**: Paper and code download, comprehensive literature review
3. **Planner**: Detailed idea design and survey review
4. **Idea Generator**: Final idea generation through collaborative innovation
5. **Assessor**: Comprehensive review for quality assessment and refinement

The clinician-engineer co-reasoning simultaneously validates medical plausibility and technical feasibility.

### Experimental Executor

- **Coder**: Code generation using medical tools like MONAI
- **Debugger**: Execution and debugging in Dockerized Sandbox
- **Analyst**: Experiment result analysis and optimization

Experiments run in isolated Docker containers with a General Implementation Toolkit standardizing medical data preprocessing, model training, and evaluation.

### Manuscript Composer

Reflects structural writing conventions of medical conferences (MICCAI, ISBI, etc.):
- **Stylist**: Determines paper structure and style
- **Formatter**: Formats to medical paper standards
- **LaTeX Compiler**: Final paper compilation

</Collapsible>

## Key Results

- **Idea quality**: Across 19 clinical tasks and 171 cases, **consistently outperforms commercial LLMs** (Gemini 2.5 Pro, GPT-5) in both LLM-based and blinded human expert evaluation, particularly in Novelty, Ethicality, and Generalizability
- **Execution success**: Significantly higher experimental success rates and implementation completeness compared to commercial LLMs
- **Paper quality**: Under double-blind evaluation by Stanford Agentic Reviewer and 10 medical experts (PhD/postdoc), generated manuscripts **approach MICCAI-level quality** and **consistently surpass ISBI and BIBM**

## Achievements and Limitations

### What's New
- **Clinical-specialized AI Scientist**: First domain-specific autonomous research framework reflecting medical evidence, specialized data modalities, and ethical policies
- **Progressive autonomy**: Reproduction → Innovation → Exploration design balances reliability and autonomy
- **MICCAI-level paper generation**: Double-blind evaluation validates that AI-generated papers reach top medical AI conference level

### Limitations
- **Author-noted**: Current framework focuses on medical imaging, time-series, and text; extension to other medical subdomains (genomics, drug discovery) is untested
- 🤖 While the evaluation shows papers "approaching MICCAI level," passing actual peer review is a different challenge from automated evaluation scores, and the clinical value and reproducibility of generated ideas require separate long-term validation
