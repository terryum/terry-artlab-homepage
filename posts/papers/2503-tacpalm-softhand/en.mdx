## TL;DR

For soft robotic hands to handle everyday objects gracefully, **rich palm contact information** is required, not just fingertip sensing. TacPalm SoftHand combines a GelSight-style visual-tactile palm (**181,000 unit/cm²** density, 1 kPa response, >5,000-cycle durability) with three two-segment fiber-reinforced pneumatic soft fingers, plus palm-finger coordination strategies for stable grasping, surface reconstruction, object classification, and dynamic pose stabilization. It achieves **100%** cup-cap pickup (vs 65% without tactile), **97%** fabric classification, and **88%** blind-bag identification.

## Problem

Adding tactile sensing to soft robotic hands has accelerated, but **almost all sensors are concentrated on fingers (especially fingertips)**. In humans, the palm carries a substantial portion of grasping/manipulation contact and is critical for stability and surface perception. Existing soft-hand palm sensors face several limitations:

- **Low sensing density**: typically under 10 unit/cm² — far below the human hand's 240 unit/cm²
- **Simple finger motions**: lack the precise grip modes needed to coordinate with the palm
- **Decoupled palm-finger design**: palm and fingers are merely combined, with little mutual benefit

The result: **bidirectional coordination** — where the palm produces rich contact signals and uses them to dynamically tune finger motions — has remained unsolved.

## Key Idea

TacPalm SoftHand integrates a **visual-tactile palm + two-segment soft pneumatic fingers + bidirectional coordination strategies**:

- **Visual-tactile palm**: micro camera + multi-layer sensing body (gray-ink-coated transparent elastomer + acrylic + diffuser) + RGB LED ring. An optical model fixes the LED mounting height at 4.56 mm to suppress inter-reflection. 1280×800 pixels over 30.08×18.80 mm yields **181,000 unit/cm²** — about 750× the density of the human hand
- **Two-segment fiber-reinforced pneumatic fingers**: each finger can independently bend in proximal-only (PA), distal-only (DA), or uniform (UA) modes — covering precision pinch through power wrap. Segment ratio L_p:L_d = 2:3 mirrors human finger proportions
- **Palm-finger coordination strategies**: closed-loop signals such as indentation contour area (ICA), edge detection, and color-difference filtering drive finger gaits (rest→touch→pull→raise→recover) and pneumatic pressures in real time

### Visual-Tactile Palm Design

The cylindrical palm integrates a micro camera module, a multi-layer sensing body, an RGB LED ring, and supporting components. The sensing body comprises (i) a transparent silicone elastomer covered with a gray-ink membrane for uniform albedo, (ii) a transparent acrylic layer for support, and (iii) a diffuser for light spreading. The LED ring is programmed into R/G/B groups to produce color gradients varying with touch depth. The optical model $h_{lm} = (r_p - w_l) / \tan(\theta/2) - h_{ca} = 4.56$ mm fixes LED mounting height to suppress inter-reflection from the acrylic layer.

### Surface Shape Reconstruction

A look-up table maps RGB intensities $(I_R, I_G, I_B)$ to depth gradients $(G_x, G_y)$, calibrated by pressing a known-diameter (5.09 mm) ball on the palm. **Fast Poisson Reconstruction** then yields the surface. Look-up accuracy is R² = 0.9783 (x), 0.9798 (y). Surface Shape Reconstruction Accuracy (SSRA), evaluated on a 1 mm-stripe block and a 0.5 mm-step triangular block, is 92.3% (x-y) and 81% (z).

### Two-Segment Pneumatic Finger

A fiber-reinforced elastomeric chamber (Dragon Skin 10, $E = 0.16$ MPa) is asymmetrically bonded to a polyethylene sheet ($E = 275$ MPa). Pressurization elongates the top while the sheet anchors the bottom, producing bending. An Ecoflex 00-30 ($E = 0.07$ MPa) outer skin provides anthropomorphic compliance. Independent proximal/distal actuation lets one finger span precision pinch through power wrap.

### Coordinated Gaits and Closed-Loop Control

- **Card pickup (Fig. 6a-d)**: A flat card on the desk is moved by a rest→touch→pull→raise→recover gait until its edge contacts the palm. Edge detection on the palm triggers other fingers to wrap adaptively.
- **Continuous flaw detection (Fig. 6e-f)**: Fingers shuffle a fabric across the palm step by step, scanning more area than the 30×19 mm sensing region. Color-difference filtering distinguishes flaw vs flawless.
- **Teapot pose adjustment (Fig. 6g-j)**: Real-time monitoring of ICA at a selected dot on the teapot bottom. When ICA drops below threshold during pouring, a finger's distal segment inflates to 80 kPa and restabilizes the teapot.

### Classification Models

Fabric and blind-bag classification use ResNet34 fine-tuned on tactile images. Per-image inference runs in under 0.01 s for real-time operation.

## Key Results

- **Palm sensing**: density **181,000 unit/cm²** (~750× human hand's 240 unit/cm²); minimum response pressure 1 kPa; >5,000 cycles without significant degradation
- **Surface reconstruction**: SSRA **92.3%** (x-y), **81%** (z)
- **Finger performance**: workspace 57.2 × 96.5 mm; max flexion PA 85.3°, DA 117.9°, UA 204.3°; fingertip force PA 0.71 N, DA 1.31 N, UA 2.19 N; max grasping force 14.6 N; min closing time 0.6 s, max speed 153°/s
- **Cup-cap pickup**: **100% success** with tactile (n=20) vs **65%** without — palm contact triggers a power-wrap mode
- **High-speed delivery**: at 180°/s and 720°/s² arm motion, tactile-enabled hand succeeds while baseline fails
- **Fabric classification**: **97%** mean accuracy across 10 fabrics with ResNet34 (under 0.01 s/image)
- **Blind-bag identification**: **88%** mean accuracy across 5 categories (hexagon nuts, peas, bolts, melon seeds, sagos); melon seeds 100%; similar-shape groups confused at ~15%
- **Dynamic teapot pouring**: ICA drop is detected during pouring and corrected by 80 kPa distal-segment inflation, restabilizing the teapot

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**
- **Paradigm shift in palm sensing density**: prior soft hands < 10 unit/cm² → **181,000 unit/cm²** (~750× human hand) while preserving 1 kPa sensitivity and 5,000-cycle durability
- **Genuine palm-finger coordination**: not mere combination — palm signals trigger finger gaits (card pickup) and fingers extend the palm's effective sensing area (continuous flaw detection)
- **Multiple applications on a single platform**: surface reconstruction, fabric/bag classification, dynamic pose stabilization, and human-robot handover demonstrated on one hardware system

**Limitations**
- Three-finger configuration — no thumb opposition or rotational dexterity, limiting fine human-like manipulation
- Pneumatic actuation requires an external compressor and multi-channel valves — challenging for mobile integration
- Palm sensing area is a 30×19 mm rectangle — small relative to a human palm, so large objects only contact partially
- ResNet34 classification requires per-scenario training — no unified general policy
- 🤖 Visual-tactile palms have high data bandwidth, and the GelSight-family sim-to-real gap remains hard — integration with learning-based policies for sim-to-real has not been verified
