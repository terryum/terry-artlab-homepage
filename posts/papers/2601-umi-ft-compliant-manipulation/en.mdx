## TL;DR

Force modulation is essential for contact-rich manipulation, but commercial F/T sensors are too expensive and bulky for in-the-wild data collection. UMI-FT integrates 2g CoinFT 6-axis sensors into UMI gripper fingers and learns an Adaptive Compliance Policy (ACP) that simultaneously controls position, grasp force, and stiffness.

## Problem

Contact-rich tasks like whiteboard wiping, lightbulb insertion, and food skewering require **precise force modulation** — insufficient force causes task failure, excessive force causes damage. However, commercial 6-axis F/T sensors (ATI Gamma etc.) are **expensive, bulky, and fragile**, making them unsuitable for large-scale data collection. UMI's in-the-wild framework captures only position and visual information, missing force data entirely.

## Key Idea

UMI-FT solves this with two innovations:

- **CoinFT Sensor Integration**: 2g, 20mm diameter, 3mm thick capacitive 6-axis F/T sensors on each finger. Captures external contact forces and internal grasp forces at 360Hz. MLP calibration (Fz error 0.58N)
- **Adaptive Compliance Policy (ACP)**: Extends Diffusion Policy to produce 21-dimensional output — reference pose (9D), virtual target pose (9D), stiffness scalar, gripper width, grasp force target. Executed through 3 parallel control loops: (1) learned policy, (2) 6D wrist admittance control, (3) grasp force admittance control

<Collapsible title="Detailed Methods">

### CoinFT Sensor Calibration

Redesigned CoinFT with oval-cross-section pillars (4mm × 0.5mm). Raw capacitance mapped to 6-axis F/T via 5-layer MLP (128-64-36-24-12, ReLU). Trained with randomized force/torque combinations (25N normal, 20N shear, 500mNm moment) using ATI Gamma as reference.

### Triple Control Loop

1. **Visuomotor policy**: CLIP ViT-B/32 for RGB, depth as grayscale, 32-timestep F/T via causal convolution. Slowest loop generating reference targets
2. **Wrist compliance**: Summed wrench from both finger sensors for 6D task-space admittance control
3. **Grasp force**: Velocity-resolved admittance regulating average CoinFT readings across fingers

### Adaptive Stiffness

Policy predicts stiffness scalar to dynamically adjust compliance level per task phase. Low stiffness (compliant) during contact approach, high stiffness (rigid) during precision insertion.

</Collapsible>

## Key Results

- **Whiteboard Wiping**: ACP **92%** (vs DP w/ Force: 28%, DP: 16%, DP w/ Compliance: 0%)
- **Zucchini Skewering (in-lab)**: ACP **80%** (vs DP w/ Force: 70%, DP: 30%)
- **Zucchini Skewering (in-the-wild)**: With in-the-wild data **20/20 (100%)**, lab-only data 4/20 (20%)
- **Lightbulb Insertion**: ACP **95%** (vs DP w/ Force: 60%, DP: 0%, DP w/ Compliance: 20%)
- **Overwhelming performance gap** over baselines lacking compliance or force sensing

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**
- First integration of **2g miniature CoinFT** into UMI for in-the-wild per-finger 6-axis force/torque collection
- **Single policy simultaneously predicting** position, grasp force, and stiffness via Adaptive Compliance Policy
- Demonstrated that in-the-wild data is critical for contact-rich task generalization (lab-only: 20% vs in-the-wild: 100%)

**Limitations**
- System **tethered via USB-serial** bridge — limits portability
- Only main camera (~80° FoV) utilized; ultrawide camera unused
- CoinFT **susceptible to delamination** under excessive tensile forces
- 🤖 Validated on only 3 relatively simple single-arm contact tasks; extension to bimanual coordination or complex deformable manipulation untested
