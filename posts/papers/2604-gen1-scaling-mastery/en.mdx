## TL;DR

GEN-0's 64% success rate was not commercially viable. GEN-1 crosses that threshold with **pretraining on 500K+ hours of human wearable data** and inference optimization, achieving **99% success rates at 3x speed** with only 1 hour of robot data per task.

## Problem

The first generation of embodied foundation models, GEN-0 (November 2025), proved that scaling laws exist in robotics, but its average 64% success rate fell far short of commercial deployment requirements. Much like GPT-2 demonstrated the promise of multitask learning but was insufficient for production use, robotics foundation models must simultaneously meet three conditions — high reliability, speed, and adaptability — to achieve "mastery." GEN-1 is the second-generation model that scales data, compute, and algorithms to cross this commercial viability threshold.

## Key Idea

GEN-1 defines **three pillars of mastery** (reliability, speed, improvisational intelligence) and is designed to achieve all three simultaneously. The key differentiator is that pretraining data comes not from teleoperation or simulation, but from **real human activities captured via low-cost wearable devices** (500,000+ hours).

Key components:

- **Wearable-based pretraining**: Pretrained purely on human physical interaction data with no robot data — adapts to robot embodiment and task simultaneously during fine-tuning
- **Redesigned multimodal architecture**: Distributed training infrastructure built from scratch to handle petabytes of physical interaction data as a first-class citizen
- **Harmonic Reasoning**: Inference optimization enabling simultaneous thinking and acting — custom kernels and paged attention for real-time inference
- **RL-based post-training**: Reinforcement learning combined with multimodal human guidance for precise, smooth control

<Collapsible title="Technical Details">

GEN-1's technical stack consists of four stages.

**Stage 1 — Large-scale Pretraining**: The base foundation model is trained on 500,000+ hours of high-fidelity physical interaction data collected from humans wearing low-cost wearable devices. This dataset contains no robot data, serving as an "existence proof that pretraining can lead to high levels of mastery without requiring large teleoperation or simulation datasets."

**Stage 2 — Architecture Redesign**: The multimodal architecture was redesigned from scratch to treat petabytes of physical interaction data as a first-class citizen. Months were invested in distributed training infrastructure and training stability.

**Stage 3 — Inference Optimization (Harmonic Reasoning)**: Custom kernels and paged attention were developed for real-time inference, enabling the model to think and act simultaneously. This maintains precision and reactivity even at higher execution speeds.

**Stage 4 — RL Post-training and Control Systems**: Post-training techniques combining reinforcement learning and multimodal human guidance refine action precision and smoothness. New hardware was deployed across new geographies to validate control systems.

</Collapsible>

## Key Results

- **Average 99% success rate** across 6 physical tasks — up from baseline without pretraining (19%) and GEN-0 (64%)
- **Up to ~3x speed improvement**: Box folding in 12.1s (2.8x vs. GEN-0/π₀ at 34s), phone packing in 15.5s (2.8x vs. GEN-0 ~43s)
- **10x less task-specific data**: Achieves comparable or better performance with 1/10th the task-specific data and fine-tuning steps vs. GEN-0
- **Only 1 hour of robot data per task** needed to reach 99% success rates

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**:
- **Commercial viability threshold crossed**: 99% success rates mark a transition analogous to GPT-3 first enabling commercial tasks like ad copywriting in the LLM domain
- **Pretraining without teleoperation**: First proof that human wearable data alone can enable robot mastery — a fundamental shift in data collection cost and scalability
- **Improvisational intelligence**: Creative recovery behaviors emerge spontaneously for out-of-distribution scenarios (regrasping washers, recovering deformable objects in unexpected configurations)

**Limitations**:
- Not all attempted tasks reach mastery-level performance — some tasks fall short of the success rates or speeds needed for practical deployment
- Emergent improvisation behaviors can be liabilities when misaligned with task objectives — an acknowledged alignment challenge
- 🤖 The model and dataset are proprietary, making independent reproduction impossible; performance claims rely on self-benchmarks
