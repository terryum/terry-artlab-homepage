## TL;DR

How should we measure VLA model performance for real deployment? Habilis-β proposes Tasks per Hour (TPH) and Mean Time Between Intervention (MTBI) as continuous operation metrics, and achieves 572.6 TPH in simulation and 124 TPH in real-world through 3-stage training + ESPADA motion compression — all running on-device.

## Problem

Existing VLA model evaluation focuses on per-episode success rates, which fail to capture **continuous operation performance** — what matters most in real deployment. How many tasks can a robot complete per hour in a factory (productivity), and how long can it operate without intervention (reliability) are far more critical in practice. Additionally, most VLA models depend on cloud GPUs, creating latency and cost barriers for real-time control.

## Key Idea

Habilis-β simultaneously achieves **Fast-Motion** and **Long-Lasting** operation:

- **3-stage training**: (1) Play data pre-training for task-agnostic interaction priors, (2) Cyclic task post-training with ESPADA to compress free-space motions, (3) Rectified flow distillation from 10+ step teacher to 2-step student
- **ESPADA**: VLM-LLM pipeline semantically segments trajectories into "casual" and "precision" phases, selectively accelerating casual phases while preserving geometric consistency
- **Prefix-Suffix architecture**: Pre-trained VLM prefix processes multimodal inputs, suffix action expert generates continuous action chunks
- **Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG)**: Deployment-time knob to dynamically balance instruction adherence and learned interaction priors

<Collapsible title="Detailed Methods">

### Flow Matching Action Expert

Learns velocity field $v_\theta(o, x_\tau, \tau)$ to match target velocity $u_\tau = \epsilon - a$, where $x_\tau = \tau\epsilon + (1-\tau)a$ is linear interpolation between demonstrated action and Gaussian noise.

Training loss: $\mathcal{L}_{FM}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{1}{Hd} \|v_\theta(\tilde{o}, x_\tau, \tau) - u_\tau\|^2_2\right]$

### Rectified Flow Distillation

Compresses 10+ step teacher to 2-step student:

$$\mathcal{L}_{distill}(\phi) = \mathbb{E}\left[\|v_\phi(o_t, x_\tau, \tau) - \frac{x_1 - x_\tau}{1-\tau}\|^2\right]$$

### ESPADA (Spatially Aware Downsampling)

VLM-LLM pipeline semantically segments trajectories into "casual" and "precision" phases, selectively accelerating casual phases. Geometric consistency guarantee: $\sum\|\Delta x'_{t+h}\| \approx \sum\|\Delta x_{t+h}\|$

### Classifier-Free Guidance

$v_{cfg} = v_{uncond} + w \cdot (v_{cond} - v_{uncond})$ with rescaling: $v_{rescaled} = v_{cfg} \cdot \|v_{cond}\| / (\|v_{cfg}\| + \varepsilon)$

## Key Results

- **Simulation continuous run (1 hour)**: Habilis-β TPH **572.6**, success rate **78.5%** (vs π0.5: TPH 120.5, success rate 47.8% — **4.75× productivity**)
- **Real-world (RB-Y1 humanoid, Dual-Bin Conveyor Packing)**: TPH **124**, MTBI **137.4s**, success rate **82.7%** (vs π0.5: TPH 19, MTBI 46.1s — **6.53× productivity, 2.98× reliability**)
- **ESPADA effect**: Increases TPH from 250.5 → 572.6 but decreases MTBI from 72.4 → 39.2 — clear **productivity-reliability trade-off**
- **RoboTwin 2.0 benchmark**: Highest reported performance at time of release on standard single-trial evaluation

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**
- Proposed **TPH + MTBI evaluation framework** for continuous operation — moving beyond single-trial success rates for real deployment assessment
- 2-step rectified flow distillation enables **on-device real-time control** — eliminating cloud dependency
- ESPADA's semantic motion compression achieves **4.75× throughput improvement** while maintaining success rates (simulation)

**Limitations**
- "Overall generalization remains limited and has not yet reached human-level task accuracy" — acknowledged by authors
- ESPADA's LLM-based segmentation may produce different results from the same demonstration, **compromising reproducibility**
- Optimal CFG guidance scale varies across tasks; learning instruction-unconditioned priors remains challenging with current robot dataset scales
- 🤖 Only validated on Dual-Bin Conveyor Packing as a single real-world task — generalization to diverse manipulation scenarios remains unverified
